10 Interns vs 1 Workstation: The Math Behind On-Prem AI Employees

A cost and capability comparison that every engineering manager should see.

The Setup

You're running a growing tech operation. You need hands on deck for monitoring, log analysis, ticket triage, report generation, infrastructure management, and documentation. The traditional answer: hire a team of junior engineers or interns.

But in 2026, there's another option. Buy a workstation, run your own LLMs locally, deploy AI agents, and let them handle the grunt work.

Let's break down the numbers.

Option A: 10 Interns / L1 Engineers (Dubai)

Annual cost per person:

  • Salary: AED 60,000 - 84,000/year ($16K - $23K)
  • Visa, insurance, laptop, desk: AED 15,000/year ($4K)
  • Management overhead (1 supervisor per 5): AED 25,000/year per person ($7K)
  • Office space, utilities: AED 8,000/year per person ($2K)

Total per person: ~AED 100,000 - 130,000/year ($27K - $35K)

For 10 people: AED 1,000,000 - 1,300,000/year ($270K - $350K/year)

What you get:

  • 10 people working 8 hours/day, 5 days/week
  • ~16,000 productive hours/year (assuming 60% efficiency)
  • Need training, supervision, HR, payroll
  • Make mistakes, take sick days, leave after 6-12 months
  • Knowledge leaves when they leave

What Can You Actually Run?

With 282GB of VRAM and 512GB system RAM, you can run:

  • GLM-5.1 (744B params, 40B active MoE) quantized locally. Beats GPT-4 on coding benchmarks.
  • Llama 4 Maverick for general tasks
  • Code-specific models for automated code review and PR analysis
  • Embedding models for RAG and knowledge retrieval
  • Multiple agents simultaneously with different roles

This isn't theoretical. We run this exact setup. Our AI agents manage infrastructure, handle emails, write content, monitor services, and respond to incidents. 24/7. The "employees" never sleep.

The Honest Caveats

The workstation doesn't replace senior judgment. It doesn't do creative problem-solving the way an experienced engineer does. It can't walk into a meeting and read the room.

But for the 80% of work that is repetitive, pattern-matched, and well-documented? It's not even close. The workstation wins on cost, consistency, and availability.

And here's the thing nobody talks about: your interns eventually become senior engineers. But in the 12-18 months that takes, you've spent AED 1-2M training them. The workstation is productive on day one.

The Real Question

This isn't about replacing humans. It's about asking: what if your senior engineers had 10 tireless assistants that never forgot anything?

That's the on-prem AI employee play. Your data stays on your hardware. Your costs are fixed and predictable. Your agents improve over time without retraining.

The workstation is available today. The interns need visas.
